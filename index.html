Erin Woo is a sophomore undergradute student studying computing at UC Santa Barbara's College of Creative Studies, which is dubbed as "a graduate school for undergrads". She is from the San Francisco Bay Area and graduated from Burlingame High School in 2017. Erin is a Girls Who Code Twitter SIP 2016 alumni and was previously a creative code apprentice for Dolby Labs and Gray Area Foundations for the Arts in San Francisco, California.

As an avid technology enthusiast and hacker, Erin's interest lies in the intersection between art, technology, and innovation. Much of her work revolves around the reconciliation between technical and aesthetic function.

    About

    During her free time, Erin enjoys attending hackathons and building upon her skillsets by working on personal projects. Besides computer science-related activities, she enjoys learning about art, culture and politics and hopes to combine these passions with her love of technology. She is also an advocate for STEM education in her local community, as seen through her involvement in her high school's robotics team and her experience teaching elementary and middle school students how to code.

    At UC Santa Barbara, Erin is a founding sister of Alpha Sigma Kappa, a sorority for women in technical studies. She was a Summer Institute of Mathematics and Science (SIMS) 2017 research intern and is currently building virtual reality applications for a dynamically haptic device in the RE-Touch lab under Prof. Yon Visell.
